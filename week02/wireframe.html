Explore Historic Lima

Take a guided tour of Lima's historic center, also known as the "City of Kings." Visit Plaza Mayor, the main square, where you can see the Government Palace, the Cathedral of Lima, and the Archbishop's Palace. Don't miss the San Francisco Monastery, famous for its catacombs containing the remains of over 25,000 people. The monastery itself boasts beautiful colonial architecture and a stunning library with ancient texts. Walk along the charming streets of the Barranco district, known for its colorful colonial buildings, art galleries, and vibrant nightlife. Don't forget to visit the Bridge of Sighs for a romantic view of the Pacific Ocean.

Indulge in Peruvian Cuisine

Lima is considered one of the gastronomic capitals of the world, so be sure to try some authentic Peruvian dishes. Visit a local cevicheria to savor Peru's national dish, ceviche, made with fresh fish marinated in lime juice, onions, and spices. Sample other Peruvian specialties such as lomo saltado (stir-fried beef), causa limeña (potato terrine), and anticuchos (grilled skewers usually made with beef heart). You can find these delicacies in traditional restaurants or trendy eateries throughout the city. Don't forget to pair your meal with a refreshing pisco sour, Peru's iconic cocktail made with pisco, lime juice, syrup, egg white, and bitters.

Visit Miraflores and the Costa Verde

Spend a day exploring Miraflores, one of Lima's most upscale and tourist-friendly neighborhoods. Take a stroll along the Malecón de Miraflores, a scenic boardwalk that offers stunning views of the Pacific Ocean. Visit Parque Kennedy, a bustling square surrounded by restaurants, cafes, and shops. This is a great place to people-watch and soak in the lively atmosphere. If you're feeling adventurous, head to Parque del Amor (Love Park), which features a massive statue of a couple embracing and offers panoramic views of the coastline.

Plaza Mayor

Visit the main square of Lima to see iconic landmarks such as the Government Palace, the Cathedral of Lima, and the Archbishop's Palace.

San Francisco Monastery

Explore this historic monastery, known for its colonial architecture, beautiful courtyards, and catacombs containing thousands of human remains.

Barranco District

Wander through the charming streets of Barranco, admiring its colorful colonial buildings, street art, and lively atmosphere. Don't miss the famous Bridge of Sighs for a romantic view.

La Mar Cebichería

Indulge in delicious ceviche and other seafood dishes at this renowned cevicheria, known for its fresh ingredients and innovative flavors.

Central Restaurante:

Experience contemporary Peruvian cuisine at Central, a world-renowned restaurant that showcases the diverse ingredients and culinary traditions of Peru.

Anticuchos Grimanesa

Savor authentic anticuchos (grilled skewers) at this popular street food spot, where you can try traditional flavors like beef heart and chicken.

Malecón de Miraflores

Take a leisurely stroll along the picturesque boardwalk of Miraflores, enjoying panoramic views of the Pacific Ocean and the city skyline.

Parque Kennedy

Visit this bustling park in the heart of Miraflores, surrounded by restaurants, cafes, and shops. Don't miss the chance to see the friendly stray cats that inhabit the park.
